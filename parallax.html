In this adaptation of the Little Mermaid, the story’s theme is shifted to the importance of taking risks and standing for your values and beliefs. Taking place in a future where storytelling is criminalized and inaccessible, secretly sharing these anecdotes of selflessness and sacrifice encourages the people enjoying this experience to come together to restore the rewarding human experience of storytelling.

The Little Mermaid

Long ago, there was a little mermaid who lived under the sea in a kingdom ruled by her father. The little mermaid and her sisters loved to sing and perform for the kingdom, but the little mermaid was still unhappy.

She knew that above the surface, another world was roaring with life. Forbidden from swimming to the surface by her father for the dangers mermaids face by humans, she dreamed of becoming one herself.

She collected human treasures in her sea grotto, imagining a world where she could dance with humans.

In an act of rebellion, she swam to the surface and watched a ship in the distance with wonderment as its crew members celebrated a Prince’s birthday with singing and dancing.

As the little mermaid quickly fell in love with the Prince, a violent storm arrived and destroyed the ship. Throwing the Prince overboard, the little mermaid saved him and swam to land to lay him on the sand.

When he awoke, he only caught a glimpse of the girl who saved his life before disappearing into the water. The only thing he remembered was how beautiful she sounded as she sang to him. He fell in love with her.


Unable to bear the truth that she could never join the Prince on land as a mermaid, she made the journey to the evil sea witch. The only solution to her problem, the witch snarled, was to drink a potion trading her singing voice for legs.

If the little mermaid didn’t succeed in making the Prince fall in love with her in ten days, she would dissolve to sea foam. Accepting this treacherous deal, she transformed to a human and set out to find the Prince.

Dizzied from her transformation, the little mermaid, now a human girl, was found by the Prince on the shore of the ocean. He took her into his palace and she became his favorite companion. Although she was mute, the Prince loved to watch her dance.

As enchanting as she was, the Prince was still in love with the girl who saved him from the storm. He was unaware that it was the little mermaid, and it pained her that she couldn’t tell him the truth.

Moving on from his fantasy of finding his true love, the Prince agreed to an arranged marriage to a Princess of a nearby kingdom, and the heartbroken little mermaid was struck with the fear of dissolving to sea foam.

Her sisters swam to the surface and offered her a sword, telling her that she could return to the sea as a mermaid if she killed the Prince with it. But she refused this offer.

Instead, the sea witch granted her an eternal soul for her selflessness, and allowed her to remain human until her natural death.
